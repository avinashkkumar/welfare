Women Entrepreneurship

1. Stand-Up India

Launched in 2016, Stand-Up India is an initiative that encourages entrepreneurship among women, SC/ST (Scheduled Castes/Scheduled Tribes) individuals, and minorities. The scheme provides bank loans ranging from Rs. 10 lakhs to Rs. 1 crore to help women entrepreneurs establish greenfield enterprises in manufacturing, services, or trading sectors.

2. MUDRA Yojana for Women (Mahila Udyam Nidhi Scheme)

MUDRA (Micro-Units Development and Refinance Agency) Yojana offers financial support to women entrepreneurs at various stages of business growth. Under this scheme, women can access loans up to Rs. 10 lakhs for establishing, expanding, or upgrading micro-enterprises and small businesses.

3. Pradhan Mantri Mahila Shakti Kendra (PMMSK)

PMMSK, launched in 2017, focuses on empowering women through community participation and entrepreneurship. It supports the establishment of Women's Self-Help Groups (SHGs), training programs, and capacity-building initiatives to enable women to become entrepreneurs.

4. Mahila Coir Yojana (MCY)

MCY is a scheme under the Ministry of Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises aimed at promoting women's entrepreneurship in the coir sector. It provides financial assistance and training to women for setting up coir-based businesses, such as coir rope making and mat manufacturing.

5. Annapurna Scheme

The Annapurna Scheme is part of the Bharatiya Mahila Bank (now amalgamated with the State Bank of India). It offers working capital loans to women entrepreneurs who run food catering businesses, food stalls, and tiffin services. This scheme supports women in the food industry.
