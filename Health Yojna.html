Health and Disease Prevention.

1. Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY)

PM-JAY, also known as the Ayushman Bharat scheme, is one of the largest government-sponsored healthcare programs globally. It provides financial protection to over 100 million families by offering health insurance coverage for hospitalization expenses, including pre-existing conditions and critical illnesses. The scheme aims to ensure that no family is left impoverished due to healthcare expenses.

2. National Health Mission (NHM)

NHM is a comprehensive health program launched to improve public health infrastructure and service delivery across India. It focuses on reducing maternal and child mortality, controlling communicable diseases, and strengthening healthcare systems at the grassroots level. Various sub-schemes under NHM address specific health issues such as immunization, maternal health, and disease control.

3. Tuberculosis Control Program (Revised National Tuberculosis Control Program - RNTCP)

RNTCP is a government initiative aimed at controlling and eradicating tuberculosis (TB) in India. It provides free diagnosis and treatment services for TB patients, including drug-resistant TB. The program also focuses on awareness and early detection to prevent the spread of the disease

4. National Vector Borne Disease Control Program (NVBDCP)

NVBDCP is dedicated to the prevention and control of vector-borne diseases such as malaria, dengue, and chikungunya. It includes measures like insecticide-treated bed nets, insecticide spraying, and community health education to reduce the transmission of these diseases.

5. Pulse Polio Program (PPI)

The Pulse Polio Program is part of India's commitment to eradicating polio. It involves mass vaccination campaigns to ensure that every child below the age of five receives the polio vaccine. The program has been highly successful in reducing polio cases in India.
